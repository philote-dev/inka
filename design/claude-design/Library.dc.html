Why does a particle in a box have nonzero energy in its ground state?
Confinement fixes \(\Delta x \lesssim L\), so uncertainty demands \(\Delta p \gtrsim \hbar / L\). A particle with exactly zero energy would have a definite momentum of zero, which confinement forbids. The floor this sets is the zero point energy,
\[ E_{1} = \frac{\pi^{2}\hbar^{2}}{2mL^{2}} \]
