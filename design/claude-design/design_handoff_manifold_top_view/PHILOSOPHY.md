# Philosophy — the top-down map

## Look down at your knowledge

The manifold answers one question — *what does my knowledge look like?* —
and the top view answers it the way a hiker reads a trail map: from above,
all at once, with names on the summits. The 3D wireframe is the emotional
projection; the top-down map is the legible one. Peaks you can stand on,
holes you can fall into, and nothing hidden behind a horizon. Users find
the shift in perspective delightful precisely because it is the same
terrain — turn the surface flat and the story stays true.

## One surface, two projections

There is exactly one data model (the `surface` object). The wireframe and
the map are projections of it, never separate illustrations. If a peak
grows in one view it grows in both. This is the honesty contract: the
manifold may be poetic, but it is never decorative.

## Honesty pairing

Every emotional mark carries its exact number beside it. Summits get a
`perf / mem` readout in the mono data voice. Gaps get a named callout with
a coverage note — "no attempts", "3 of 41 seen" — not a vague shadow.
The map seduces; the numbers keep it honest.

## Gaps are holes, not shame

Missing knowledge renders as literal absence: the surface tapers to the
base plane and the rim is a quiet dashed line. No red, no warning icons.
A named hole with "no attempts" reads as an invitation — somewhere to go
next — and tapping it starts the drill. Low coverage leaves the surface
visibly incomplete rather than smoothed over; we never fake terrain we
have no data for.

## Borrow from cartography, not dashboards

Topographic maps earned centuries of trust, so the map speaks their
language: index contours every third level, summit markers, leader-line
callouts, a datum point in each depression, a legend that decodes every
mark. What it refuses: chart chrome, axes, gridlines, tooltips-as-legend.
It should feel like a survey sheet of the user's mind, not analytics.

## The reserved color language

Amber is memory, blue is performance, lilac is readiness — nowhere else in
the product may use these hues. Region tint comes from the glow sources,
so each dome carries its leading statistic's color, and label text inherits
a softened version of its region hue. Everything interactive stays
monochrome; color is data, never decoration.

## Calm at every size

The map is the small-screen default because it stays legible where the
wireframe cannot. Density scales down gracefully: fewer contour levels,
callouts collapse to summit dots, the legend folds into the drill sheet.
The quiet warm neutrals, hairline strokes, and generous margins are the
same at every size — the map is a place to breathe, not a scoreboard.
