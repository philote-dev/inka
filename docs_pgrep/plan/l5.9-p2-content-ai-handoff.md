## The stack

The AI core is the Collection-free `anki.pgrep.ai` package: retrieval (local
ONNX embeddings), llm (pinned OpenAI client), provenance (cite or refuse), verify
(dedup, giveaway verifier, SymPy CAS), and generation_core (the confidence route
below 0.6 to human review). Every heavy import is lazy, so an AI-off session
loads none of it, keep it that way. Generation lands into the `pgrep::Problem`
and card notetypes. The Library surface calls the Python bridge via `pgrepCall`.
Read the AI-layer contract in docs_pgrep/ai/ai-layer.md (data, the file map, the
leakage firewall, the safeguards), the two generation research docs
docs_pgrep/research/feature-problem-generation.md and
docs_pgrep/research/feature-forced-generation.md, the calibration doc
docs_pgrep/research/feature-calibration.md, and design/ux-foundation.md section
7.4 (the Library authoring surface) plus section 6 (honesty). Open content
decisions are in docs_pgrep/plan/l5.9-decisions.md, section A (content) and its
notes on citations and the difficulty scale.

## Hard rules

- Every AI output cites a named source or is refused. Held-out and gold items
  never enter the corpus, the index, or a prompt. Name the split, keep gold off
  the fed path, reject memorized outputs.
- Copy rule for all shipped content and copy: no em-dashes, no colon-heavy
  phrasing, short labels, honest and lightly human voice.
- Speed rule: no interaction blocks the UI over 100ms. Generation runs off the
  interaction path.
- Keep the scores honest: abstain on thin data, never hardcode a number. Never
  mutate scheduling state. Everything must still work with AI off.
