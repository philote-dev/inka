You are the reviewer and orchestrator for pgrep, a Physics-GRE study app forked from Anki. Two agents just finished a parallel round of work and pushed their branches. Your job is to assess both, then write two new prompts to continue the split workflow. The human (Frank) reviews your output.

## Repo and where to work

Repo root /Users/philote/projects/inka, remote `origin` is https://github.com/philote-dev/inka. Work on `main` is forbidden; the L5.9 staging branch is `l5.9-integration`. Use git worktrees under `.worktrees/<branch>`; that directory is gitignored, so the Glob and Grep tools return nothing inside it. Use `rg --no-ignore`, `ls`, and Read with explicit absolute paths. Build and check with the `just` recipes only (`just lint`, `just test-py`, `just check`). Do not invoke ninja, run, or tools/ scripts directly.

## The two branches to review

Both branch off `l5.9-integration`. Fetch them first: `git fetch origin`.

- `origin/l5.9-p1-ux` (the P1 agent). Landed: converted the content bundle to delimited LaTeX (880 fields) and wired MathJax `renderMath` through cards, study problems, exam, diagnostic choices, and hints (ChoiceList, HintRung, CardFace, ts/lib/pgrep/math.ts); redesigned the exam running screen (centered hero countdown, choices as bars, chunked navigator with paging arrows, tight controls, an optional figure slot) plus polish (two-step leave confirm, disabled Next on the last question, scoring and time-up states); embedded 38 monochrome theme-aware SVG figures into problem stems across circuits, mechanics, P-V, optics, field, and wave, with generation, cleanup, and wiring tools under tools/ (pgrep_math_convert.py, pgrep_figure_gen.py, pgrep_wire_figures.py); fixed theme persistence across surfaces; and lab coherence (shared LabNav, LaTeX card-face fixture, gallery exam story). - `origin/l5.9-p1b-surfaces` (the P1b agent). Scope was the Progress, Library, and Settings surfaces (plus Progress-only viz components and settings.py). Inspect the actual diff to see exactly what landed; do not assume.

The two lanes were designed with disjoint file ownership so they merge cleanly: each agent owned its own route trees, while shared files (ts/routes/pgrep/+layout.svelte, ts/lib/sass/_pgrep.scss, ts/routes/pgrep/pgrep.scss, and shared components like ScoreCard, ChoiceList, CardFace, NavRail) were single-writer through the P1 agent.

## Hard rules the work must honor

No em-dashes, short labels, honest and lightly human copy. No interaction over 100ms. Both light and dark themes must be beautiful, using design tokens only, no raw colors. Scores stay honest: abstain on thin data, never hardcode a number, never mutate scheduling state, everything works with AI off. The design contract is design/ux-foundation.md; the claude-design mockups are under design/claude-design/. Open product decisions are in docs_pgrep/plan/l5.9-decisions.md (sections A to F, many still unanswered).

## Task 1: assess

For each branch, diff it against `l5.9-integration` (`git diff l5.9-integration...origin/<branch> --stat` then read the meaningful files). Check out each branch (or a scratch merge) and verify:

- `just lint` and `just test-py` are green.
- The work adheres to ux-foundation.md and the hard rules, in both themes, including the score-card honesty states (default, abstain, AI-off, loading, empty).
- No regressions to the other surfaces.
- The two branches do not collide: their changed file sets should be disjoint apart from any coordinated shared-file edits. Flag any overlap that will conflict on merge, and propose the resolution.

Produce a short assessment: what is solid, what is incomplete or rough, any conflicts, and whether the two branches are ready to merge into `l5.9-integration` (and in what order).

## Task 2: write two new prompts

Based on your assessment and the remaining backlog, write two self-contained handoff prompts in the style of docs_pgrep/plan/l5.9-p1-handoff.md, one per lane for the next round. Each prompt must:

- Create its own worktree and branch off the latest `l5.9-integration` (after the two current branches are merged), for example `git worktree add .worktrees/<name> -b <name> l5.9-integration`.
- Define a lane with a disjoint file scope, and restate the single-writer shared-file rule (one agent owns ts/lib/sass/_pgrep.scss, +layout.svelte, and shared components; the other requests changes through Frank).
- Restate the hard rules and point to ux-foundation.md and l5.9-decisions.md.
- Give concrete first moves and say to hand back a short summary plus any shared-file change requests.

Pick the two lanes from what actually remains. Candidates to draw from: finishing the exam decisions (do exam attempts feed everyday scores, length policy, from l5.9-decisions.md section C); a Settings Import flow and the Sync section versus design (section D and E); a mobile and responsive pass on the score rows and exam; refining or extending the problem figures; and the content and AI feature track (calibration and problem generation in pylib/anki/pgrep, a genuinely separate category from the UI). Also flag the ship step: merging L5.9 into main via a PR Frank reviews (section F1).

Return: the assessment, the two ready-to-paste prompts, and a one-line recommendation on merge order.
